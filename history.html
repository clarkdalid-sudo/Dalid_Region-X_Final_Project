Brief History

Northern Mindanao has long been a crossroads of indigenous communities and trading routes. Its islands and inland highlands developed unique cultures shaped by local indigenous groups and later Spanish and American influences. Agriculture, fishing, and small-scale mining historically supported lively local economies.

During the 20th century the region grew as modern transport and commerce connected coastal cities like Cagayan de Oro and Iligan with interior provinces. Today, tourism, agriculture, and services drive the regional economy while many communities work to preserve ancestral traditions and natural landscapes.
